Main Tracks

Research Directions and Papers

I am most interested in topics that connect method design, system behavior, and evaluation logic.

Direction 01

Visual representation under appearance variation

Studying how visual evidence breaks and reorganises under occlusion, clothing change, cross-modal imaging, and in-the-wild viewpoints in Universal ReID — covering both UniGeo's reliability-aware 3D geometric injection and the TPAMI appearance-variation survey and methodology.

Direction 02

Generation–verification decoupled structured scientific reasoning

Letting a small model own generation while a validator, compiler, and issue-list repair own correctness. PEARL turns ARCHE's scientific reasoning graph extraction into a schema-first, verifier-guided training framework.

Direction 03

Multi-agent systems for real tasks

Guided mobile interaction for ambiguous requests, A2A-protocol multi-agent workflows, and modular robot communication — treating evaluation protocol, observability, and engineering closure as first-class design goals.

ECCV 2026 Submission 2025.10 - 2026.03

Reliability-Aware 3D Geometric Injection for Universal Person Re-identification

Treats monocular 3D human geometry in Universal ReID as conditionally-used structural evidence rather than an always-trusted auxiliary: SMPL parameters are kinematically decoupled, a consistency-aware gate predicts when the 3D signal is reliable, and a residual dual-stream fusion prevents bad 3D from contaminating the 2D backbone — validated across 9 benchmarks.

SMPL kinematic decoupling reliability-aware gate residual dual-stream fusion 9 benchmarks
Planned EMNLP Submission 2026.03 - present

PEARL: Decoupling Generation from Verification for Small-Model Scientific Reasoning Graph Extraction

A schema-first framework for Peircean scientific reasoning graph extraction on ARCHE: structure learning is decoupled from DOT-syntax generation, and diagnosis is decoupled from repair. A 4B Qwen student is trained on the graph_spec IR with code-based validator, compiler, and issue-list repair, backed by 5-teacher quality-weighted SFT and a smart prompt — targeting majority-teacher superiority on joint-score.

graph_spec IR validator + compiler issue-list repair 5-teacher quality-weighted SFT joint-score majority superiority
TPAMI Survey Preparation 2026.03 - present

Appearance Variations in Person Re-Identification: A Survey

Rebuilds the taxonomy of appearance variation along short-term, long-term, and compound branches, and reorganises existing methods with a robustness-vs-generalization framework; the long-term axis explicitly separates clothing-state, beyond-clothing, and deployment-distribution evolution. §2–§8 are drafted with 231 references; §1 Introduction and citation alignment are still iterating.

three-branch taxonomy robustness vs. generalization §2–§8 drafted 231 references
